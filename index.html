Sigiriya Lion Rock

Sigiriya or Sinhagiri (Lion Rock Sinhala: සීගිරිය) is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka. A huge granite column that is approximately 180 meters (590 feet) high dominates this site of historical and archaeological significance.

Yala National Park

Yala () National Park is Sri Lanka's second largest and most popular national park, located near the Indian Ocean. Three of the five blocks in the park are now accessible to the public. Kumana National Park (Yala East) and Lunugamvehera National Park are both located in close proximity to each other. Palatupana (Block 1) is one of the blocks with individual names The Southern Province and Uva Province form the southeastern region of the country where it is located. About 300 kilometers (190 mi) from Colombo, the park covers 979 square kilometers (378 sq mi). The first two designated national parks in Sri Lanka were established in 1900 with the designation of Yala as a wildlife sanctuary and Wilpattu as the first two. The park is widely recognized for its diverse wildlife and plays a critical role in protecting Sri Lankan elephants, leopards, and aquatic birds. ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Rated by TripAdvisor

Bentota Beach

Bentota is a coastal town in Sri Lanka, located in the Galle District of the Southern Province. It is approximately 65 kilometres (40 mi) south of Colombo and 56 kilometres (35 mi) north of Galle. Bentota is situated on the southern bank of the Bentota River mouth, at an elevation of 3 metres (9.8 ft) above the sea level. The Bentota Beach is a wonder for tourists. A tropical climate with high temperates throughout the year, but a natural sight for everyone. ⭐⭐⭐⭐Rated by TripAdvisor

Nuwaraeliya

Nuwara Eliya (Sinhala: නුවර එළිය [nuwərə ɛlijə]; Tamil: நுவரெலியா) is a city in the hill country of the Central Province, Sri Lanka. Its name means "city on the plain (table land)" or "city of light". Nuwara Eliya District's administrative capital is located in the city, which boasts a picturesque landscape and temperate climate. I felt at home when I was at Nuwaraeliya. ⭐⭐⭐⭐Rated by TripAdvisor

Adam's Peak

Adam's Peak is a 2,243 m (7,359 ft) tall conical sacred mountain located in central Sri Lanka.[1][2] It is well known for the Sri Pada (Śrī Pāda; Sinhala: ශ්‍රී පාද, "sacred footprint"), a 1.8 m (5 ft 11 in) rock formation near the summit whose name is also used for the mountain itself. ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐Rated by TripAdvisor
